details:

Type: Illustration

Completed: August 15, 2021

Tools: Procreate, Ipad, Apple Pencil

Service: Illustrator

goal:

To create an illustration using a lotus flower pattern.

I had never used patterns or repetition before so this project allowed me to try something new. I wanted to think outside the box and force myself into a new element. This would also help me in other design aspects as repetition and patterns can be seen everywhere.

challenges:

The greatest challenge was seamlessly incorporating a lotus flower pattern into my artwork.

I had my initial goal but I didn’t know how I wanted to develop it into a complete idea. How do I use patterns? What can I make with a lotus flower pattern? How do I use a pattern to create something greater than the sum of its parts? To answer these questions I decided to think about what a lotus flower means to me.

Process:

The process was smooth, I only had to rework my idea once. After the rework, the rest of the piece came together without issue.

When I think of a lotus flower, I'm reminded of Asian culture, the color light pink, and water. Focusing on these three things my ideas began coming together. The Ying-yang symbol is a staple of Asian culture and it's very simple, I thought to somehow make the Ying-yang symbol with the lotus pattern. I initially wanted to create a bonsai tree out of lotus patterns and incorporate it into the Ying-yang symbol. Once I started I realized that it would have looked messy and complicated. Messy and complicated are feelings that contradict what I feel when I think of a lotus so I decided to scrap the bonsai idea. After playing with the lotus pattern and the Ying-yang symbol, I created a shape that reminded me of twin dragons. I colored the two dragons red and blue to keep with the theme of balance that comes from the Ying-yang symbol. The overall style of the drawing is made to look like an ink or water painting, falling in line with the initial ideas I had about what lotuses make me think of.

results:

The art piece was very successful, the different aspects of the piece came together nicely to paint a picture of balance.

From this piece, I learned to take a step back and rework my ideas. I’ve gained a bit of experience working with rules that I set for myself. I wanted to create something with a lotus flower pattern and I achieved what I set out to do. I’d like to explore ink and water art styles more as this was my first time making something like this.
