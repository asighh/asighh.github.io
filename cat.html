details:

Type: sculpture

Completed: February 27, 2024

Tools: Air Dry Clay, Scupting Tools, Water

Service: Sculptor

goal:

To create a sculpture of a cat.

When I made my first sculpture, the lotus, I felt that the difficulty came from all the details in the petals that make up the entire structure. I decided to do a cat next because it would be simpler as the fine details are mostly in the paws and the head. It's not that I want to avoid difficult sculptures but I'd like to practice how accurate I can make my sculptures.

challenges:

Similar to my first sculpture my stamina when kneading and handling the clay is not very high.

There was less experimenting during this sculpture, it felt easier and more straight forward.

Process:

I didn't make an armature.

I decided not to make an armature to see how a sculpture without one would turn out.

Process:

I was lucky with this sculpture because I had a willing model.

Having the ability to look at an actual cat and see her features in real time really helped.

Process:

Working on the cat's facial features and paws felt similar to drawing.

I used a sculpting tool for the finer details and my hands when I needed to smoothout and blend in the clay.

results:

The sculpture looked like a cat and I was happy with it.

Picking a simpler structure helped ensure that the final product came out how I thought it would. The little experience I gained from my first sculpture also helped a lot. I'm excited to start my third and continue delving into 3d art. I still have a lot of room to improve.
