goal:

As an illustrator, my goal was to create cover art for my song “WONDERLAND”.

As a songwriter and artist, my goal was to create a song that touches on my desire to escape.

After completing the lyrics for the song I decided to create artwork for it. I wanted the artwork to tell the story of the song with one picture. According to Fred R. Barnard (1921), “one picture is worth a thousand words.”

Additional Thoughts:

“I don't see color I only see shades my vision black and white in a daze. I see black I see white I remember the times I was down down bad exhausted”.

I bring my vision back up again to emphasize how I perceive the world. In the beginning of the song I said I saw in red and blue but now at the end of the song I see in black and white because after feeling intense anger and sadness instead of dealing with those emotions, its easier to just cut them off. It also has a second meaning because before TV was in color it was in black and white and in the line I talk about remembering my past.

challenges:

In terms of writing lyrics, It’s not challenging because it's just the thoughts that have been on my mind put together rhythmically.

The main challenge was, using the words from the song to create a visual representation.

The title of the song is, “wonderland”. I was set on creating some type of magical environment that could represent a wonderland. The issue was my lack of experience in drawing environments and architecture.

Process:

The song was recorded on a website called Soundtrap.

The process was straightforward. I kept having ideas and building on them.

I did not have problems once I started. I had already been thinking about the concepts for a while. The lyrics in the song had been in my mind for a long time, allowing my ideas time to form. My thoughts kept flowing as I was working on the piece. There were two lines in the song that heavily influenced the art. One was, “I see red, I see blue, I get sad, angry too” the second one was, “I need a trip to my perfect wonderland, could call me wander man”. These two lines gave me a lot of room to create as they mean a lot to me. “I see red, I see blue, I get sad, angry too”, is about my negative emotions that are constantly tinting my perception of the world. It's hard to enjoy things when there’s a constant blue or red filter influencing your vision. “I need a trip to my perfect wonderland, could call me wander man”, this line is about escaping from the problems in reality. One of my strengths or weaknesses is I overthink things a lot and I can get lost in my thoughts. My mind is both an escape and a cage, hence the isolation I created by putting the environment in a snow globe and in between two hands.

results:

The artwork was successful in creating an isolated wonderland that someone can escape to.

I tried to incorporate the lyrics by putting the wonderland inside of an eye. Eyes are the windows to the soul and I wanted to tap into that thought by making this eye the window into wonderland. I also made it a circular shape to make someone think of a snow globe, to emphasize the isolated wonderland, inside its ecosystem.
